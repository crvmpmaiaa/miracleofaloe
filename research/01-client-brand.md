## Observations — The Opportunity

1. **The brand palette is actually good** (aloe-green + deeper green) — but the current site spams it everywhere, so it reads "generic health store" instead of "premium botanical."
2. **No serif typography** anywhere — which means the 40-year heritage story has no visual anchor. Adding a considered serif unlocks the "editorial heritage" positioning.
3. **Logo is a horizontal wordmark + aloe plant** — works in header but may need a simplified plant-only mark for compact placements. Favicon already gives us this.
4. **Trust signals already live** (4.9 stars, 40 years, Walmart) — just not merchandised well. The new site needs to lead with these.
5. **Tone shift target:** From "friendly health store" → "family-owned Texan heritage brand with proprietary science." Warm, grounded, premium-without-corporate. Think Aesop's warmth + Vermont-farm earnestness, at a Walmart-accessible price point.

## Hero Product Recommendation

**Miracle Foot Repair® Cream 32oz with pump** is the clearest hero for the landing-page demo:
- Literal #1 best-seller (listed first in both Best Sellers AND Favorites)
- Trademarked → proprietary formula story to tell
- $36.99 premium price point justifies a landing-page-grade conversion experience
- Classic DTC landing page fit: before/after dry/cracked heels, ingredient deep-dive, 40-year-formula heritage, problem/solution arc
- Foot & Hand is the category they own — owning it harder is the wedge

Strong alternatives: **Aloe All Over®** (broadest mass appeal), **Miracle Leg Calming Cream** (currently being promoted, novel angle), **Ultra Aloe Juice** (best vehicle for UltraAloe® process storytelling — but less emotional than cream).
